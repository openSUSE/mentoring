Why should I become a mentor?


Because the world needs mentors and open source needs more code. Mentoring is important for the future of open source software and especially openSUSE. It introduces young developers to the culture and practices of open source.

Be a mentor because we need people involved with openSUSE to make a difference in people’s’ lives and in open source. We need mentors to share their experiences and encourage future generations of open source developers.


What makes a good mentor?

  • Time, time and time!
  • Being a FOSS evangelist and already part of an openSUSE project!
  • Have a clear vision of your mentoring project!
  • Having strong organization skills because you have to manage your Mentee and your daily life.

We carefully picked some guidelines for mentoring! Please read them for more information about becoming a mentor: Guidelines by FLOSS manuals, Mentoring.org, Gnome mentoring guidelines, KDE mentoring guidelines.

Okay, let's go!

Is your openSUSE project already listed on our projects site? If not, please contact first our team and ask to add your project. Let's create awareness about our past, present and future projects!

Go to our GitHub repository and add a new issue for your project. Please follow our recommended template!

Label the issue with your projects name (e.g. Machinery) and assign it to yourself. It will now magically appear on our project site!

Wait till a Mentee gets in touch with you!
